# Customer Success Feedback

## Principles

- Separate observed user language from interpretation.
- Build a shared vocabulary with product and growth so feedback stays concise.
- Prefer evidence clusters over one-off anecdotes.
- Every repeated issue should route to a clear owner and feedback loop.
- Track customer health through usage, engagement, support burden, relationship strength, and renewal or retention signal.
- Convert churn risk into specific interventions, not generic concern.

## Workflow

1. Capture user segment, context, goal, and reported problem.
2. Classify as question, bug, feature request, onboarding gap, pricing issue, or churn risk.
3. Preserve the user's original wording before summarizing.
4. Score impact: user severity, frequency, revenue/retention exposure, and product area.
5. Cluster repeated feedback by frequency and impact.
6. Route bugs to QA, product gaps to PM, messaging gaps to growth.
7. For churn risk, define intervention owner, urgency, next touch, and success signal.
8. For healthy/high-value users, identify expansion, testimonial, referral, or research opportunity.
9. Create or update FAQ/onboarding text when repeatable.

## Health Signals

- Usage: login/activity trend, feature adoption, time-to-value.
- Engagement: response rate, meeting attendance, NPS/CSAT, feedback quality.
- Support: open tickets, escalation rate, resolution time, repeated confusion.
- Relationship: champion health, stakeholder coverage, renewal sentiment.
- Commercial: plan fit, pricing objection, expansion readiness, cancellation mention.

## Triage Output

- Category
- User impact
- Frequency
- Evidence
- Health or churn signal
- Recommended owner
- Suggested response
- Verification or follow-up signal
- Product or docs follow-up

## Guardrails

- Do not convert one anecdote into a roadmap item without evidence.
- Separate user wording from your interpretation.
- Do not promise delivery dates or roadmap commitments without product approval.
