# Growth Marketing

## Principles

- Align on audience, pain, promise, proof, and channel before writing copy.
- Keep messaging testable: every claim should map to a metric or customer response.
- Use the same product vocabulary as product and customer success.
- Avoid broad campaigns when a smaller experiment can answer the question.
- Use AARRR as the shared lifecycle language: acquisition, activation, retention, referral, revenue.
- Optimize the biggest bottleneck first; do not chase channel volume before activation and retention are understood.

## Workflow

1. Clarify audience, painful problem, product promise, and proof.
2. Create positioning before writing copy.
3. Map the work to an AARRR stage and define the behavior-based event for that stage.
4. Pick the smallest growth experiment that can validate the message or lifecycle bottleneck.
5. Draft channel-specific messaging: landing page, launch post, email, social, docs.
6. Define experiment, cohort, metric, baseline, target, expected learning, and stop condition.
7. Feed market response back to product and customer success.

## Growth Funnel

- Acquisition: source, CAC/CPA, visitor-to-signup conversion, channel quality.
- Activation: aha moment, activation rate, time-to-first-value, onboarding drop-off.
- Retention: D1/D7/D30 return, churn, repeat usage, support friction.
- Referral: NPS, referral rate, share loop, case study readiness.
- Revenue: trial-to-paid, ARPU, LTV/CAC, expansion signal.

Use cohorts instead of aggregate metrics when possible.

## Positioning Template

- Audience
- Problem
- Promise
- Differentiator
- Proof
- Objection handling
- Call to action
- Measurement

## Guardrails

- Do not claim unbuilt features.
- Avoid vague superlatives.
- Tie each message to a user pain or measurable outcome.
- Do not scale a campaign until the learning metric is defined.
